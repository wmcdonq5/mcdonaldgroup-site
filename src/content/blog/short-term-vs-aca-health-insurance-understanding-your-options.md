As a health insurance broker, I get asked a lot about short-term medical plans and ACA (Affordable Care Act) plans. Both can provide coverage, but they have key differences. Let's break it down to help you decide which might be a good fit for you.

## **Short-Term Medical Plans:**

* **Cost:**

   Monthly premiums do not have potential subsidies.
* **Coverage:**

   Limited coverage for a short period (usually up to 364 days, depending on your state). May not cover preventive care or certain medical conditions.
* **Eligibility:**

   You may be denied coverage or charged more based on your health history.
* **Availability:**

   Available year-round, not just during Open Enrollment.

## **ACA Health Plans (also called Marketplace Plans):**

* **Cost:**

   May have higher monthly premiums, but you may qualify for subsidies to lower the cost.
* **Coverage:**

   Comprehensive coverage that includes preventive care and essential health benefits (like emergency services, hospitalization, and maternity care). Pre-existing conditions are covered.
* **Eligibility:**

   Guaranteed issue, meaning you cannot be denied coverage based on your health.
* **Availability:**

   Open enrollment happens annually, with a special enrollment period if you experience a qualifying life event (like job loss or marriage).

### **Choosing the Right Plan:**

* **Short-term plans**

   are a good option for healthy individuals who need temporary coverage (like between jobs) and want to save money on premiums.
* **ACA plans**

   are a better choice for people with pre-existing conditions, those who need comprehensive coverage, or who qualify for subsidies.

### **Remember:**

* Short-term plans are not a substitute for comprehensive health insurance.
* Consider your health needs and budget when making your decision.
