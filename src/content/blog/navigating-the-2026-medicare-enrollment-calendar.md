## Navigating the 2026 Medicare Enrollment Calendar

### 1. Initial Enrollment Period (IEP): Your First Chance

For most people, this is a **7-month window** around your 65th birthday.

* **When it happens:**

   It starts 3 months before your 65th birthday month, includes your birthday month, and lasts for 3 months after.
* **Why it matters:**

   This is your primary opportunity to sign up for Medicare Parts A and B, a Medicare Advantage plan (Part C), or a Prescription Drug plan (Part D).
* **The Risk:**

   Missing this window may result in 

  **lifetime late enrollment penalties**

  .

### 2. General Enrollment Period (GEP): A Second Opportunity

If you missed your IEP and don't qualify for a special period, you can still sign up for Original Medicare during the GEP.

* **Dates:**

   January 1 – March 31 each year.
* **Coverage Start:**

   Your coverage will begin the first day of the month after you sign up.
* **Note:**

   You may still face late enrollment penalties for delaying Part B.

### 3. Annual Enrollment Period (AEP): Your Yearly Check-Up

Also known as Open Enrollment, this is when you can re-evaluate and change your current coverage.

* **Dates:**

   October 15 – December 7.
* **Changes Allowed:**

   Switch from Original Medicare to Medicare Advantage, change Advantage plans, or add/drop a Part D drug plan.
* **Effective Date:**

   Any changes made during this time take effect on 

  **January 1, 2026**

  .

### 4. Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment (MA OEP)

If you already have a Medicare Advantage plan, this window gives you a second chance to make a single change.

* **Dates:**

   January 1 – March 31.
* **Changes Allowed:**

   You can switch to a different Advantage plan or return to Original Medicare.

### 5. Special Enrollment Periods (SEPs): For Life's Big Changes

You may qualify to change your coverage outside of standard windows if you experience a major life event.

* **Common Reasons:**

   Moving to a new area, losing employer coverage, or qualifying for "Extra Help".
* **New for 2026:**

   A temporary SEP is available if you chose a plan based on incorrect network information in the online Plan Finder tool.
