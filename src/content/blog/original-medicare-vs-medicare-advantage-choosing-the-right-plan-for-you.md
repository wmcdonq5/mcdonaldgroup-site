Turning 65 and eligible for Medicare? You might be feeling overwhelmed by all the options. One of the biggest decisions you'll make is between staying on Original Medicare and joining a Medicare Advantage Plan. This blog will break down the key differences to help you pick the plan that best fits your needs.

## **Understanding the Basics**

* **Original Medicare:**

   This is the traditional government-run program with two main parts: Part A covers hospital stays, skilled nursing facility care, hospice care, and home healthcare. Part B covers doctor visits, outpatient care, and preventive services. You'll typically need to pay a monthly premium for Part B and a deductible for both parts. You can choose any doctor or hospital that accepts Medicare.
* **Medicare Advantage Plans (Part C):**

   These are offered by private insurance companies approved by Medicare. They must cover all the services that Original Medicare covers (Parts A and B), but they can offer additional benefits like vision, hearing, dental, and gym memberships. Plans often have features like networks of doctors and hospitals you must use, and may have out-of-pocket maximums that limit your costs for covered services in a given year.

### **Choosing the Right Plan**

* **Flexibility:**

   Original Medicare offers more freedom to choose any doctor or hospital that accepts Medicare. Advantage Plans offer more predictability in costs with out-of-pocket maximums, but may limit your network choices.
* **Extra Benefits:**

   Consider if vision, dental, hearing, or gym memberships are important to you. Many Advantage Plans offer these, while Original Medicare generally doesn't.
* **Costs:**

   Compare monthly premiums, deductibles, and copays for both options. Factor in your anticipated healthcare needs and how much you're comfortable paying out-of-pocket.

**Remember:** You can always switch between Original Medicare and a Medicare Advantage Plan during the annual Open Enrollment Period (October 15 - December 7).
